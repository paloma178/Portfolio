Drive Ability Opening Doors Program

Home Projects

The Challenge

Funded by the Ministry of Labour, Training, and Skills Development (MLTSD), the Drive Ability—Opening Doors program provides students, recent graduates and apprentices from various academic disciplines with paid work placements in the automotive and advanced manufacturing sectors. Work placements can last between 10 weeks and up to four months. Employers are eligible to hire specialists from various programs (HR, Marketing, CNC Machining, Engineering, Computer Programming etc.) The challenge was to collect eligible employers and participants to apply to the program.

The Journey

I created a Social Media Ad campaign that supports the lead conversion to the Career Ready Fund. LinkedIn was the most indicated social media platform to perform this campaign. Students/Grads are on this site to better their careers, find a new job, connect with business, and so on. While platforms like Facebook have lots of personal demographics, LinkedIn specializes in career-related filters that can optimize our effectiveness on this campaign. For this project, we have also built a Landing Page that contains all the campaign information.

My Role

I played the strategist and executer role for this campaign. I provided recommendations and best practices in order to increase the landing page views and conversions.

The Outcome

The goal for this campaign were to create awareness and generate qualified leads. This campaign still ongoing.
Website: georgebrown.ca/drive-ability
